Saint-André-les-Vergers Poster Game | Adobe Illustrator | Personal

  • Context: solo

Presentation

The city of Saint-André-les-Vergers had organized a competition where we had to create a poster to promote the event "Saint-André in game". Without hesitation, I launched Adobe Illustrator and got to work!

Creation Process

In the space of an afternoon (5-6 hours), I created my poster. Here's the result:

Well, spoiler alert: my poster didn't win. But that's okay! I was able to practice while having fun. So, I'll reveal the behind-the-scenes of the creation!

For this poster, I didn't try to be original at all costs. My goal was to practice by reproducing designs that I liked. Like a fusion of inspirations found online!

For example, in the game "Thank Goodness You're Here", at one point in the game, the hero accidentally flies into the air holding a balloon. I love this scene, which presents a calm and peaceful situation, and I wanted to transcribe it onto my poster... And that's what I did!

Obviously, I didn't just take a screenshot. I redrew everything, while keeping the base colors and given style!

Then, I added details to fill my poster, starting with simple balloons:

I used the shape builder tool to create shadows (for reference, this tool allows merging or cutting objects in contact,) nothing too complicated! Then I had the idea to add an airplane with a banner displaying information, like the date or entry price.

I then created other small elements, like chess pieces or cards, using the pen tool to trace them from models. Easy and effective!

At this stage, I just had to add the information, export the image... and it was done!

Trivia

If you take a look at my layer list, you'll see that I took particular care to properly organize and rename objects. (I love organizing my projects, it makes everything so much nicer and cleaner!)

Conclusion

In summary: this project was super fun to create! Even though I didn't win, I'll surely try my luck again in a future competition. And this time, I'll try to be a bit more original in my designs, now that I better master Adobe Illustrator!
