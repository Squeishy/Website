School Project - Utopia Project | Adobe Illustrator | University

  • Context: SAE, solo

Presentation

In SAE 103, we were asked to design and create a new logo and a new graphic charter for the Utopia cinema in Troyes. The objective was to propose a visual identity that aligns with their history, clientele, and the services they offer.

To achieve this, we had to develop a graphic charter and create numerous sketches as well as a moodboard to design a logo that was relevant and appropriate, but more modern and better anchored in our time.

Basic Observations

Let's start by understanding our subject by visiting the cinema's website.

Needless to say that the website and logo have a somewhat dated look, but nevertheless maintain all the codes of the old era: the use of a handwritten look on the website, retro colors like red or brown, and especially a completely non-responsive website.

Thus, in my logo, I will want to keep this somewhat retro aspect while keeping in mind that we need a more modern logo!

Creating the Moodboard

My moodboard, although imperfect, helped me find my colors, ideas, and graphic style. I wanted something related to space, a recurring theme in old movies.

Creating Sketches

To begin, I took the time to analyze several logos of independent cinemas to identify current graphic trends. I then embarked on a series of sketches, seeking to find a balance between the retro side of the Utopia cinema and a more contemporary touch. My first ideas were a film reel... and a lemon?

I really liked the logo in the top right and decided to continue in this direction, avoiding "the lemon that had no business being there", according to my professor. (In hindsight, he wasn't wrong.)

After creating four other versions of the logo I liked, I achieved a really convincing result: the film reel at the top first represents cinema, but also the retro aspect of the place. The banner around the logo is a film reel where the word "Utopia" is inscribed. Finally, the sun below gives a planetary aspect to the cinema and confers a "worldwide" dimension, while also sparking children's curiosity with the space theme!

From Paper to Screen

This transition wasn't complicated, however, finding the right font proved to be a bit more challenging: we wanted a cinema style while incorporating a "newspaper" aspect to the logo, which I hadn't mentioned earlier to maintain some suspense. Thus, I found two fonts that I decided to keep:

  • Playfair Display SC offers an elegant, modern, and sophisticated look.
  • MKSansserifLightTallX Regular brings a modern and clean style, perfect for secondary text.

Once I found my fonts, I finalized the logo using Adobe Illustrator, without encountering any difficulties. The most complicated part was creating the film reel, which, in reality, only took five minutes. It was just a matter of creating a special brush and applying it to a line. The rest of the logo is composed of simple shapes, assembled using the Shape Builder tool. And here's the result!

A light version and a dark version were also created, as requested in the exercise.

The 12 Tasks of Jordan

In addition to the moodboard and logo, we had to create other works:

  • The logo's safety zone, which corresponds to the size of the white part of the film reel.
  • The favicon, the small logo that appears in the website tab, featuring a pixelated version of the logo while maintaining its main details.
  • Three pictograms on the theme of cinema with a vintage touch, respecting a design grid.

These had to respect a grid, which you can see here:

  • Several mockups including the logo in different contexts and products, created in Photoshop. They allow visualizing the integration of the logo in various media (special mention to the cap, which I really like!)

The "mo-cups": cups featuring the cinema, but also because they're mockups, "mo-cups", cups, in English...

  • The photographic direction, which I kept very simple: a desaturated sepia. To achieve this effect, we apply a black and white filter to the image, then add a yellow tint. This brings a warmer and more welcoming atmosphere, while recalling old newspapers printed in black and white, which corresponds to the vintage and welcoming aspect we were looking for.

The Little Details

One thing that sometimes disappoints me in people's work is the lack of fun or personal little details. I like my work to be instantly recognizable, rather than resembling everything that's already been seen. That's why I always integrate little secrets in my projects to make them unique!

For example, the example text for the fonts isn't the traditional "Lorem Ipsum", but rather "Chatus Gepetus", a reference to ChatGPT!

In addition to the "hilarious mo-cups", I modified the logo on the last page to add a final fun touch: the Futurama characters have repainted the logo. Also, Fry and Bender (the red-haired man and the robot, to the right of the logo) are holding paint bombs in their hands.

The purpose of these little secrets isn't to be mocking, but rather to bring a touch of fun to my work, making the exam more enjoyable for the professor. It transforms the correction into a kind of Easter egg hunt!
