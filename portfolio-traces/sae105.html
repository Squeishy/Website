Presentation

During SAE 105, we were asked to design and create a website on a free topic, which had to use JSON files to store values and PHP files to retrieve them. Everything was organized on 6 pages that we'll look at, as they all have something special. Especially the "secret page"...

The index page

The index page has nothing special: it's a page that serves as a hub and redirects to all other pages. The only interesting thing we can notice on this one is a 3D plastic duck that can be rotated. But how did I do that? Well, with the Three.js module! Once imported into the site files, it's just a matter of modifying the code a bit so that the part that displays the image takes the shape we want. Once that's done, everything is good and ready to use!

However, something important is missing... The 3D model of the duck. Yes! I downloaded a model from Sketchfab, then converted it to the .gltf format, which is the type of model supported by Three.js.

Once my duck was imported, I just had to code 2 or 3 additional lines to prevent the duck from moving, and that was it! I had a super cool 3D duck! ‚ò∫Ô∏è

The data page

The data page had to contain a table of values to be retrieved from a JSON file using the dataTables module. Since I had already used JSON in my games and on my personal website to manage translations, this was child's play here. It was just a matter of reading a bit of the dataTables documentation and the table was ready to use. I don't have much more to say about this, as it was, in reality, quite simple.

The gallery page

In the gallery page, we were asked to design a system that would take images and then integrate them into the site. Only conditions: they had to be in .jpeg format and not exceed 200 ko.

Many people simply modified the image size via Gimp or Photoshop, or even using a website. But I find this approach long and boring, especially when we have the possibility to code a solution!

That's exactly what I did. First, we had to take the image and convert it to JPEG format, which is easily done with the line of code:

imagecreatefromjpeg($img);

Once the image was converted, it was just a matter of putting it in a loop to reduce its size until it weighed less than 200 ko!

For uploading the image, we just had to follow the course instructions, which was done without any problems! (It was just a matter of saving it in the site files.)

The contact page

The contact page honestly has nothing special. Again, it was just a matter of following the course and using the mail() function to send an email by retrieving form data. I'll go over it quickly, as I didn't do anything particularly original, just following what was asked of me.

I also take this opportunity to mention that I created a JavaScript file that makes small plastic ducks fall in the background, which makes the background a bit less empty!

The partners page

This page was also meant to be used to display the partners of our fictional company created in another SAE. It's therefore simply a page with our first and last names, and not much more, apart from a few texts.

However, I still took the liberty of adding a fun feature: when you hover over one of our heads, the image flips to display a "funny" photo of us. I don't know why, but I always love making improbable faces in my photos... It's probably going to cost me my future job one of these days.

The credits page

The credits page, which was requested, groups all the links to the resources I used, such as fonts, images, and 3D models. It remains very simple.

Alright! Well, let's talk about the other page!

One of our professors told us that "it was okay to put a fun little easter egg." So, being the smart guy I am, I decided to really take this suggestion at face value and add "a fun little easter egg" to my website.

Here's how it works: when you reload or change pages three times, a popup appears with the following message:
"Hot ducks in your area", accompanied by a "sign up" button.

You're probably starting to guess where this joke is going to take us...

By clicking the button, you're redirected to another page. And there, I hope for you that it's not familiar. No need to draw a picture: it's a parody of "that famous website".

By clicking the "I am a duck - Enter" button, you'll have access to a whole bunch of duck videos! And I even took the time to make all the thumbnails, it was quite fun to do!

To go even further in my silliness, I created a fake advertisement for a pill "that could make your duck grow about 20 times bigger." I even used Photoshop to remove the original duck from a photo and paste another one on top. Well, I must admit that it's a bit noticeable, especially because of the grass in the background that moves.

This fake advertisement was so well done that it was being blocked by my own ad blocker! I had to be clever and change its name in the HTML file so it would display correctly (yes, we're at that point).

You're probably wondering: "But how did he manage to have a website that looks so much like the original?" Well, believe it or not, I managed to find an image where all the explicit content of the site had been replaced by cute ones... Yes, the little "yellow TicTacs" from Despicable Me. Even the texts had been adapted, which was perfect for me. I just had to copy everything.

Finally, to top it all off, when you click on a video, the user is redirected to a video I had made myself. In this video, I directly address our professor telling him that "it's not good to go on sites like that while grading papers".

It could have stopped there... But no! This joke went in front of the entire amphitheater, with its 150 students!

I've never been so scared in my life, but everyone burst out laughing. It's a golden memory that I'll keep precious until my grave!
